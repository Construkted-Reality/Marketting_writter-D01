The mapping world is heading down the same path, and we're here to interrupt that timeline.

The geospatial market research tells us that **85% of user complaints about current mapping solutions involve collaboration challenges**. Why? Because these platforms were designed for individual consumption, not community contribution. They're digital territories owned by corporations, not shared spaces belonging to the people who inhabit them.

## The Technology That Makes This Possible

Now, let's get a bit technical (because we love this stuff). The Construkted Globe isn't just a pretty interface—it's built on some serious infrastructure that makes user-generated content actually work at scale.

**Smart File Management**: Remember when we talked about those massive point cloud datasets? Our platform handles files ranging from hundreds of megabytes to hundreds of gigabytes, automatically optimizing them for web viewing without sacrificing detail. We're talking about processing 47 million data points down to web-friendly formats while maintaining millimeter accuracy.

**Universal Format Support**: Whether your scan came from a drone, a terrestrial laser scanner, photogrammetry software, or a smartphone, we can handle it. E57, LAS, LAZ, IFC, RVT—we've got you covered. No more format conversion headaches or software licensing nightmares.

**Progressive Loading**: Zoom into any location on the globe, and you'll see details loading progressively. Start with a broad view, then dive deeper as more data becomes available. It's like peeling back layers of an onion, but each layer reveals more human stories rather than corporate marketing.

**Community Curation**: Here's where it gets really interesting. Our community doesn't just passively consume content—we actively curate it. Users can rate, comment on, and contextualize scans, creating a living knowledge base that grows more valuable over time.

## The Restrictions That Actually Make Sense

Let's address the elephant in the room: yes, we do have restrictions. But they're not the kind that serve corporate interests—they're the kind that serve community values.

**Real Places Only**: Every scan must represent an actual location on Earth. No fictional worlds, no fantasy landscapes, no corporate-generated "test environments." Why? Because we're building a digital record of our actual world, not a playground for digital art projects.

**Public Interest**: Content must serve some form of public good—preservation, education, environmental monitoring, cultural documentation, or community planning. We're not here to host someone's 3D scan of their living room (unless it's part of a larger documentation project).

**Respectful Engagement**: We have clear guidelines about scanning culturally sensitive sites, private property, and areas requiring permission. This isn't about restricting access—it's about ensuring that our digital commons serves everyone equitably.

These restrictions exist to maintain the integrity and value of the platform, not to extract maximum profit from user-generated content.
