# Construkted Reality Brand Guidance Document

## 1. Brand Identity & Core Mission

**Company Name:** Construkted Reality

**Tagline:** "Democratizing 3D Data for Everyone"

**Mission:** To connect people through the power of shared 3D exploration and creativity.

**Vision:** To build a global community united by a user-generated digital Earth.

**Core Problem We Solve:** We eliminate data fragmentation, inaccessibility, and collaboration barriers for anyone working with geospatial 3D data—from global enterprises to individual creators.

---

## 2. Brand Positioning & Value Proposition

### Market Position
Construkted Reality occupies the **critical white space** between prohibitively expensive enterprise GIS solutions ($25,000+ annually) and inadequate free tools that lack collaboration features. We serve the **$2.25-3.6 billion underserved market** of small teams (2-10 people) who need professional-grade capabilities with consumer-grade ease-of-use.

### Unique Value Propositions

**For Professionals (AEC, Surveying, Urban Planning, Environmental Consulting):**
- "A powerful, scalable engine to streamline complex workflows, improve stakeholder communication, and reduce costly rework"
- Professional GIS capabilities without the $25,000/year enterprise price tag
- Real-time collaboration that eliminates email attachment chaos and manual version control
- ROI: $72,000-144,000 annual productivity savings from workflow automation

**For Hobbyists & Creators (Artists, Explorers, Tech Enthusiasts):**
- "An accessible canvas to bring creative visions to life"
- Zero technical barriers—manage 3D data from any web browser without special tools
- Share discoveries with the world and contribute to a global digital record
- Connect with a community that shares their passion for 3D exploration

### Key Differentiators
1. **Web-based accessibility:** No special software installation required
2. **Collaboration-first:** Google Docs-style real-time editing for 3D geospatial data
3. **Transparent pricing:** Flat-rate team pricing without aggressive sales calls
4. **Open-access philosophy:** Public assets showcased on the Construkted Globe
5. **Dual-audience focus:** Equally valuable to professionals and hobbyists

## 4. Brand Voice & Messaging Framework

### Tone Characteristics
- **Conversational & Enthusiastic:** Like chatting with a knowledgeable friend over coffee
- **Inclusive & Democratic:** "We" and "our" language emphasizing community and shared mission
- **Transparent & Humble:** Share failures openly; celebrate discoveries without hype
- **Educational yet Exciting:** Balance technical depth with accessibility

### Voice Guidelines

**DO:**
- Use first-person plural ("we," "our") to signal shared journey
- Show enthusiasm grounded in real capabilities: "This reduced processing time from 4 hours to 8 minutes"
- Quantify impact with before/after metrics
- Share transparent decision-making: hypothesis → solution → outcome
- Use analogies for complex concepts: "GIS data layers stack like transparent maps"
- Celebrate user contributions and community achievements

**DON'T:**
- Use corporate jargon or marketing buzzwords
- Lecture or moralize—treat readers as peers
- Overstate capabilities or promise unrealistic outcomes
- Create artificial barriers to entry or gatekeep knowledge
- Focus solely on features without explaining user benefits
- Make unsubstantiated claims without concrete evidence

### Messaging Hierarchy

**Primary Message:**
"Open-access, web-based 3D data collaboration for everyone—no special tools or expertise required"

**Supporting Messages:**
1. Professional capabilities at accessible pricing
2. Real-time collaboration without versioning complexity
3. Manage massive datasets from any web browser
4. Join a global community building a user-generated digital Earth
5. Transparent pricing with generous free tier

## 8. Legal & Compliance Guidelines

### Trademark Usage
- **Construkted Reality™:** Always use correct spelling (not "Constructed")
- First mention in content: Include trademark symbol (™)
- Subsequent mentions: No symbol required
- Capitalization: Always capitalize both words

### Product Name References
- **Assets:** Capitalized when referring to the specific product feature
- **Projects:** Capitalized when referring to the specific product feature
- **Construkted Globe:** Always capitalized, always both words

### Claims & Substantiation
- **Quantified claims:** Must reference market analysis data or customer evidence
- **Savings claims:** Use conservative estimates from market research ($72,000-144,000 range)
- **Market size claims:** Reference "$2.25-3.6 billion underserved opportunity"
- **Comparison claims:** Only compare to publicly documented competitor pricing

### Prohibited Content
- Disparaging competitors by name (use "enterprise solutions" or "legacy tools")
- Unsubstantiated performance claims without metrics
- Guarantees of specific ROI without "typical" or "up to" qualifiers
- Promises of features currently in development without clear "coming soon" disclaimer

### Data Privacy & Security
- Emphasize: "Your data remains yours—we never claim ownership"
- Compliance mention: "Enterprise-grade security and compliance features available"
- Transparency: "Public Assets on Construkted Globe are user-opted contributions"

## 9. Visual & Format Guidelines

### Content Structure Preferences
- **Conversational headers:** Descriptive and semantic, not template-based
  - Good: "Why processing 500 GB of LiDAR data stalls on a single core"
  - Bad: "Introduction," "Methodology," "Results"

- **Progressive disclosure:** Core idea first, then layered details in subsequent sections
- **Scannability:** Short paragraphs (3-5 sentences), bullet points, bolded key terms
- **Length:** 1,000-2,500 words for blog posts (unless specified otherwise)

### Visual Placeholders
- Include descriptive placeholders: "[Insert diagram: 3D point cloud before/after processing]"
- Emphasize before/after comparisons for impact
- Use real data visualizations where possible, not generic stock imagery

### Technical Content Standards
- **Show the work:** Accompany claims with concrete artifacts (code snippets, data tables, command examples)
- **Quantify impact:** Always pair techniques with before/after metrics
- Example: "PDAL filtered 47 million points to 12 million, reducing processing from 4 hours to 8 minutes"

## 10. Competitor Reference Policy

### Acceptable References
- Generic categories: "enterprise GIS solutions," "traditional desktop tools," "legacy platforms"
- Market context: "Solutions requiring $25,000+ annual investments"
- Feature gaps: "Tools lacking real-time collaboration" (without naming)

### When Naming Competitors IS Allowed
- Factual comparison tables with public pricing data
- Integration announcements: "Construkted Reality now integrates with [Tool]"
- Migration guides: "Switching from [Tool] to Construkted Reality"
- Industry analysis: "The geospatial market dominated by Esri and Autodesk"

### When Naming Competitors is PROHIBITED
- Negative comparisons or disparagement
- Unsubstantiated claims about competitor limitations
- Highlighting competitor failures or security issues
- Emotional or subjective language about competitor products

## 12. Success Metrics & Content Quality Standards

### Engagement Metrics
- Blog posts: 3+ minute average read time, 40%+ scroll depth
- CTAs: 2-5% click-through rate for awareness content, 10-15% for decision-stage content
- SEO: Rank within top 10 for target keywords within 90 days
- Social: 5%+ engagement rate (likes, shares, comments)

### Quality Standards
- **Readability:** Flesch Reading Ease score of 50-70 (accessible to general audiences)
- **Accuracy:** All quantified claims referenced to market analysis or documented sources
- **Originality:** Zero tolerance for generic "best practices" content—every post must offer unique insights
- **Brand Consistency:** Voice and messaging aligned with this guidance document

### Red Flags (Content Should NOT Be Published If)
- Uses corporate jargon or buzzwords excessively
- Makes unsubstantiated claims about savings or performance
- Fails to quantify impact with concrete metrics
- Lacks concrete examples, artifacts, or evidence
- Feels like a sales pitch rather than educational content
- Doesn't clearly serve one of our two audience segments

When generating content for Construkted Reality, the AI should:

1. **Always consider dual audiences:** Balance professional and hobbyist perspectives
2. **Reference market context:** Draw from the $2.25-3.6B market opportunity and documented pain points
3. **Quantify everything:** Use market analysis data for credibility (70% cost savings, $72K productivity gains)
4. **Show competitive advantage:** Position against expensive enterprise tools and limited free alternatives
5. **Emphasize community:** The user-generated digital Earth is a core differentiator
6. **Be transparent:** Share decision-making process, acknowledge limitations, celebrate failures as learning
7. **Maintain enthusiasm without hype:** Ground excitement in real capabilities and user outcomes

This brand guidance should be applied consistently across all content generation to ensure Construkted Reality's message remains authentic, compelling, and differentiated in the geospatial SaaS market.
