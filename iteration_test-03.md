## The Corporate Mapping Problem (And Why It's Broken)

Let's be honest: when it comes to digital Earth mapping, we're living in a world where a handful of tech giants control how we see our planet. Google, Microsoft, Apple—they've built incredible platforms, but they're also the gatekeepers. They decide what gets mapped, how detailed it can be, who gets access, and what you can do with the data once you have it.

**The numbers tell a sobering story**: Esri's ArcGIS costs $25,000 annually for a typical 5-person team. DroneDeploy runs $17,940-35,940 per year. These aren't just software licenses—they're permission slips to participate in how humanity documents its physical world.

But here's the thing: **the most interesting, valuable, and meaningful 3D data isn't captured by satellites or corporate fleets**. It's the detailed scans of that ancient temple your archaeology team documented last month. The precise stockpile measurements your construction crew captured last week. The intricate heritage site your local historical society scanned before the old mill gets demolished.

This is the data that matters. And right now, it's scattered across hard drives, trapped in proprietary formats, or locked away in expensive corporate systems.

## How It Works: The Technical Magic (Made Simple)

Under the hood, we've built some pretty sophisticated infrastructure to make this work seamlessly:

**Smart Large File Management**: Your massive point cloud scans? We handle them with progressive loading and chunked processing, so whether you're viewing a detailed industrial site or a simple building scan, everything loads smoothly.

**Universal Web Viewer**: Upload any format—E57, LAS, LAZ, IFC, RVT—and we automatically convert it to a web-viewable format. No special software required. No downloads. Just point, click, and explore in any browser.

**Project-Based Collaboration**: Your scans become part of collaborative workspaces where teams can layer multiple datasets, add annotations, take measurements, and communicate—all without altering your original files.

**Automatic Version Control**: Every upload creates a version snapshot. See exactly how a site evolved over time. Compare different survey dates. Build historical timelines.

But here's the beautiful part: **you don't need to understand any of this** to participate. Upload your data, and the platform handles the rest.
