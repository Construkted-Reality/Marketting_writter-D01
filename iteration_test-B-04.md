## The Corporate Mapping Monopoly We're Breaking Free From

Here's the thing about current digital mapping: it's owned. Every street view, every satellite image, every 3D model lives behind corporate walls with terms of service, data policies, and profit motives that don't align with community interests. When Google maps your neighborhood for Street View, they own that data. When Bing creates 3D models of cities, Microsoft controls how you can use them.

**We're building something radically different.**

The Construkted Globe isn't just another mapping platform—it's humanity's first truly democratic digital Earth. Every pixel, every point cloud, every 3D model belongs to the community that created it. No hidden algorithms deciding what gets prioritized. No corporate overlords deciding who gets access to what data. Just a living, breathing digital mirror of our world, built by the people who actually inhabit it.

## Why This Matters Now (And Why We Can't Wait)

The timing couldn't be more perfect. **3D scanning technology has reached the tipping point** where anyone with a smartphone can contribute meaningful data. Drone technology is accessible. Even professional-grade LiDAR scanners are dropping in price monthly.

Meanwhile, corporate mapping is becoming more restrictive, not less. Privacy concerns are making companies pull back public access to detailed imagery. Commercial interests are determining what gets mapped and what doesn't.

**This creates a once-in-a-generation opportunity** to establish an alternative—a mapping platform owned by its contributors, governed by community values, and focused on preservation rather than profit.
