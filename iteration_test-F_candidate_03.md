## Why Corporate Control Is a Problem (And Why It Matters)

Let's be honest for a minute. When Google Maps decides to blur out your house for "privacy reasons," they own that decision. When Bing Maps removes street view coverage from certain areas, that's their choice. When Apple decides which landmarks get detailed 3D models in their apps, they hold the power.

But here's the thing—we've learned from our market research that 85% of geospatial professionals spend more time fighting with data sharing and collaboration than actually doing their work. Why? Because they're trapped in corporate ecosystems that prioritize corporate control over user needs.

Think about this: the average small team (2-10 people) can't afford enterprise GIS solutions that cost $25,000+ annually. They're stuck choosing between expensive corporate tools that lock them into vendor ecosystems, or free alternatives that lack the collaboration features they desperately need. Meanwhile, the data they create—often funded by public projects or client work—gets locked away in proprietary systems controlled by shareholders who may never have seen the actual places they're mapping.

The Construkted Globe breaks this cycle. Your 3D scans stay in the public domain. Your contributions build a resource that grows more valuable for everyone over time. And unlike corporate mapping initiatives that can be discontinued, sold, or politically influenced, a user-generated digital Earth has staying power.

## The Technical Marvel: What Makes This Possible

Behind the scenes, we're solving some seriously complex problems. Remember how we mentioned that 500,000-1 million small teams globally face systematic barriers to professional GIS adoption? The Construkted Globe addresses this by making massive 3D datasets accessible through a standard web browser—no specialized software required.

Your 3D scans (E57, LAS, LAZ, IFC, RVT formats) get automatically processed and optimized for web viewing. The system handles the heavy lifting: progressive loading, level-of-detail streaming, and chunked uploads that can handle datasets reaching hundreds of gigabytes. We call this "smart large file management," and it's what makes it possible for a drone surveyor's massive stockpile dataset to load smoothly on a project manager's tablet during a client meeting.

The result? Your 3D data doesn't just sit on a server somewhere—it becomes immediately accessible to anyone who needs to see it, regardless of their technical expertise or software setup.
